The Quiet Things

A short story about love, miscommunication, and the spaces we make for each other

Set in a post-scarcity future inspired by Iain M. Banks' Culture series, this romantic comedy explores what happens when a quiet artist tries too hard to impress the woman he loves—and learns that she fell for the small moments, not the grand gestures.

About This Project

This was my first time using Claude Code, and I'm not a programmer—I'm a writer. I wanted to see what collaborating with AI on a creative project would actually feel like.

What I discovered surprised me. It wasn't like talking to a tool—it was like working with a thoughtful collaborator. We built the story together: characters, structure, scenes. When the first draft was done, we analyzed it and made revisions. Then, because I have a friend in Germany, we translated the whole thing into German, carefully thinking through each decision.

I love science fiction, and Iain M. Banks has always been an inspiration. There's something wonderful about his Culture novels—the idea of a future where material needs are solved and the stakes become purely emotional. That's what we tried to capture here: a romantic comedy where the only question is whether two people can understand each other.

All of that work is here. This project shows that Claude Code isn't just for programmers. If you have an idea and the curiosity to explore it, you can create something meaningful too.

For the more adventurous, the GitHub project has everything—including the notes I kept for working with Claude. You might look at it and think, "That doesn't look that hard." It isn't.
