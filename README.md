# The Quiet Things

A short story set in a post-scarcity future, inspired by Iain M. Banks' Culture series.

## The Story

Tomas is a quiet artist who creates immersive experiential pieces—spaces you walk through, inhabit, feel. Veda is sharp and curious, drawn to sincerity over spectacle. When Tomas tries to impress her with a grand public gesture, he misreads her overwhelmed silence as rejection and flees across the solar system. She chases him down to ask why he ever thought he lost her.

**Theme:** Love doesn't require grand gestures. She fell for him watching him work quietly, not for the spectacle.

## About This Project

This was my first time using Claude Code, and it started as a learning experiment. I'm a writer, not a coder, and I wanted to see what collaborating with AI would actually feel like.

What I discovered surprised me. It wasn't like talking to a tool—it was like working with a thoughtful collaborator. We built the story together: characters, structure, scenes. When the first draft was done, we analyzed it and made revisions. Then, because I have a friend in Germany, we translated the whole thing into German, carefully thinking through each decision.

I love science fiction, and Iain M. Banks has always been an inspiration. There's something wonderful about his Culture novels—the idea of a future where material needs are solved and the stakes become purely emotional. That's what we tried to capture here: a romantic comedy where the only question is whether two people can understand each other.

All of that work is here. Feel free to read it, share it, adapt it. It's all released under CC0—I claim no rights to any of it. Honestly, Claude Code did the heavy lifting. How much credit could I really claim? But that's part of the point: this project shows how easily someone can create something meaningful with AI collaboration.

I'm so glad I gave it a try.
